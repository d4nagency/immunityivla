## Understanding NAD+ and Why It Matters

NAD+ is arguably one of the most important molecules for human health and longevity. This coenzyme plays a critical role in over 500 enzymatic reactions, making it essential for virtually every cellular process in your body.

**Cellular Energy Production:**
NAD+ is fundamental to cellular respiration—the process by which your cells convert nutrients into ATP (adenosine triphosphate), the energy currency of your body. Without adequate NAD+, your cells cannot produce sufficient energy to function optimally, leading to fatigue, brain fog, and diminished physical performance.

**DNA Repair and Cellular Maintenance:**
Every day, your DNA suffers thousands of damage events from normal metabolic processes and environmental factors. NAD+ activates sirtuins and PARPs—enzymes responsible for repairing DNA damage and maintaining cellular integrity. As NAD+ levels decline with age, this repair capacity decreases, contributing to cellular aging and dysfunction.

**Mitochondrial Health:**
Mitochondria are the powerhouses of your cells, and they require NAD+ to function properly. Declining NAD+ levels lead to mitochondrial dysfunction, which is now recognized as a hallmark of aging and a contributor to many age-related diseases.

**The Age-Related NAD+ Decline:**
Research shows that NAD+ levels decline by approximately 50% by age 40 and continue to decrease as we get older. This decline correlates with many symptoms we associate with aging: decreased energy, cognitive decline, reduced metabolism, and slower recovery from exercise and illness.

**Why IV Delivery Is Essential:**
While oral NAD+ supplements exist, they face significant absorption challenges. The digestive system breaks down much of the compound before it reaches your bloodstream, and cellular uptake is limited. **NAD+ IV therapy** delivers this vital coenzyme directly into your circulation, bypassing digestive barriers and ensuring maximum bioavailability. This direct delivery allows for the high doses necessary to meaningfully replenish cellular NAD+ levels.

Most side effects are mild and temporary. Some clients experience mild chest tightness, abdominal discomfort, or nausea during the infusion, which typically resolves by slowing the infusion rate. Our nurses monitor you closely and adjust the flow to maximize comfort.

Oral NAD+ supplements and precursors (like NMN and NR) face significant absorption barriers in the digestive tract. IV delivery bypasses these barriers completely, delivering NAD+ directly to your cells at concentrations impossible to achieve with oral supplements.
