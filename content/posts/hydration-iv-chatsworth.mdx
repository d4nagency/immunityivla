## Frequently Asked Questions — Chatsworth Hydration IV Therapy

**How do I know if I need hydration IV therapy?**
Common signs of dehydration include: thirst, dark urine, fatigue, headache, dizziness, dry mouth, and decreased urine output. If you're experiencing these symptoms, especially after outdoor activity or heat exposure, IV hydration can provide rapid relief.

**How quickly will I feel better?**
Most clients report significant improvement within 30-60 minutes of starting treatment. Full hydration benefits are typically realized within 2-4 hours.

**How long does a hydration IV take?**
The infusion typically takes 30-45 minutes for one liter of fluids. From arrival to departure, expect approximately 60 minutes for your complete appointment.

**Is hydration IV therapy safe?**
Yes, when administered by licensed professionals. Our California-licensed RNs and NPs conduct health screenings before treatment and monitor you throughout. Sterile, single-use equipment ensures safety.

**Can I get treatment at Stoney Point or another outdoor location?**
While we typically treat clients at their homes for comfort and safety, we can sometimes accommodate outdoor locations. Contact us to discuss your specific needs.

**How often should I get hydration IV therapy?**
This depends on your activity level and needs. Active outdoor enthusiasts might benefit from weekly treatments during peak season, while others may only need occasional sessions during illness or after intense heat exposure.

**Can equestrians get treatment at their stables?**
Yes, we regularly serve clients at their ranches and stables. We understand the equestrian lifestyle and can work around barn schedules and animal care needs.

**What's the difference between drinking water and IV hydration?**
When you're dehydrated, your digestive system may not absorb fluids efficiently. IV hydration bypasses the digestive tract entirely, delivering fluids directly to your bloodstream for 100% absorption and immediate cellular availability.

**Do I need a prescription for hydration IV therapy?**
No prescription is needed for basic hydration IV therapy. Our licensed clinicians can assess your needs and provide appropriate treatment during your appointment.
