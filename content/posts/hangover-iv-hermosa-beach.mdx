## Understanding the Science of Hangovers

**Hangovers aren't just about drinking too much — they're a complex physiological response.** Alcohol acts as a diuretic, causing your body to lose fluids and electrolytes at an accelerated rate. It also triggers inflammation, irritates your stomach lining, disrupts sleep patterns, and depletes essential nutrients including B-vitamins, magnesium, and glutathione.

In Hermosa Beach's coastal environment, these effects are often amplified. The combination of sun exposure, salt air, physical activity, and ocean swimming creates pre-existing dehydration that alcohol consumption worsens. A mild night out in a cooler climate might result in a manageable morning-after; in Hermosa Beach, the same consumption can leave you feeling completely depleted.
