EEAT Editorial Policy - Immunity IV LA

Purpose: Ensure all published posts demonstrate Experience, Expertise, Authority, and Trustworthiness (EEAT) for health/wellness topics.

Before Publish – EEAT Gate:
- Author Bio: Post must include author byline with credentials (e.g., MD, NP, licensed clinician, industry expert). If no credential, provide a credible author note.
- Credible Sources: Include 1-2 credible sources cited with links. Prefer official orgs, peer-reviewed content, or established authorities.
- Local Relevance: Include 2-3 local landmarks or neighborhood references; tailor content to the local market.
- Disclosures: Add any necessary disclaimers for medical or legal claims; avoid definitive medical directives.
- Privacy & Safety: Include privacy statement links; ensure no sensitive data disclosed.
- Canonical/Schema: Ensure canonical URL, meta, and article/schema markup are set.
- Internal Linking: Hub linking: service pages -> city posts -> related posts.

Post-Publish – Ongoing:
- Update author bios with current credentials when they change.
- Audit a sample of posts monthly for EEAT adherence.
- Maintain an Editorial Policy page and link from the footer.
